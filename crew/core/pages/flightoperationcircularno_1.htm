These are some highlights that we expect you to comply during approach and landing phase:

1. Decelerated Approach: 

  • Decelerated approach with AP or AP/FD guidance is the standard flying technique for ILS approach and approaches using FINAL APP guidance
  • Any approach other than aforementioned should be flown with early stabilized approach technique.

2. Final Landing Configuration:

  • Strongly recommended to be fully configured at 1500ft to avoid unstable approach

3. Stabilized Approach Criteria:

  • Stabilized approach criteria must be met at 1000ft AAL, if its not mandatory go around must be carried out.
  • Criteria of stabilized approach are: 
    • The aircraft is on the correct lateral and vertical flight path.
    • The aircraft is in the desired landing configuration.
    • Thrust is stabilized, usually above idle and the aircraft is at target speed for approach.
    • Speedbrake retracted.
    •  Landing checklist has been completed.

4. Long Landing and/or Long Flare:

  •  Avoid to “de-stabilize” the approach below 200ft AAL in order to catch up with the glide path.
  • During the approach try to keep aiming point all the way and landing within the touchdown zone.
  • For A320 flare height is around 30 feet during normal circumstances, it may differ due to environmental conditions (tailwind, upslope runway, etc)
  • Though is not recommended to do so, adjusting vertical speed to smoothen touch down rate its on your discretion.
  • However, If long flare is occur and pass the last touchdown zone marking, go around must be carried out to avoid runway overshoot.
